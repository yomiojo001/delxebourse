A gift card (also known as gift certificate in North America, or gift voucher or gift token in the UK) is a prepaid stored-value money card usually issued by a retailer or bank to be used as an alternative to cash for purchases within a particular store or related businesses.

From the purchaser’s point of view, a gift card is a gift, given in place of an object which the recipient may not need, when the giving of cash as a present may be regarded as socially inappropriate or a prepaid payment card that contains a specific amount of money available for use on a variety of electronic payment purchases.

Physical gift cards are actual physical (plastic) cards and may also be redeemable both in-store and online too.

eGift cards are not actual cards, they are cards in digital form, electronically delivered directly to the purchaser’s designated destination. It may also be redeemed both in-store and online too.

Printable vouchers are a fast alternative to physical gift cards, but require printing. Similar to an eGift card, vouchers are electronically delivered directly to the purchaser’s designated destination.
